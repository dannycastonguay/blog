SUBJECT: Master the Art of Effective Email Communication

Are your emails hitting the mark? We've just published a new article to help you write emails that are clear, concise, and impactful. Here's a sneak peek:

- **Start with the Point**: Get straight to the ask, decision, risk, or update. No suspense, just clarity.
- **Bad News First**: If something's wrong, say it early. Honesty saves time.
- **Brevity Wins**: Every word should count. Cut the fluff and get to the point.
- **Be Literal and Specific**: Avoid sarcasm and generalities. Use names, numbers, and facts for precision.
- **Single Focus**: Each email should have one main purpose. Multiple topics? Send multiple emails.
- **Clear Next Steps**: Specify who needs to do what, by when. Make it actionable.
- **Facts, Judgment, and Recommendations**: Keep them distinct. If uncertain, say so.
- **Forward-Friendly**: Ensure your email makes sense even without added context.
- **Avoid Generic Language**: Keep it human and specific to your context.
- **Time Respect**: Keep emails short enough to be read on one screen.
- **Clean Threads**: New topic, new email. Only CC those who need to know.
