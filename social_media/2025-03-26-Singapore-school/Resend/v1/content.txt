SUBJECT: Why Our School Experience is a Game-Changer

We recently attended a parent-teacher meeting at our kids' school, and it was a breath of fresh air compared to our experiences in the U.S. The environment was more rigorous, yet brimming with potential for innovation.

Our observations? The kids here are polite and a bit reserved, which mirrors a culture of respect. This is a welcome change from the unruly behavior we often saw in U.S. schools. Classrooms are peaceful, fostering an atmosphere ripe for academic growth. Gone are the days of fashion-driven hierarchies and aggressive disruptions.

A highlight we can't ignore is the upcoming school camp – two nights and three days of outdoor adventure! It reminded us of our own cherished childhood experiences with overnight trips. Why don't U.S. public schools do the same? It seems to boil down to logistics and cost. Here, teachers are certified for activities like rock climbing, ensuring a safe and supervised experience.

As we reminisced, I indulged in a turmeric latte – the fanciest menu option. Surprisingly delightful, it was buttery and frothy, sans coffee, and packed with antioxidants.
